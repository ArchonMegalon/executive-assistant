# Designer Feedback Split: EA / skill layer

Source: 2026-03-08 Chummer designer market scan. Distilled direction: community demand is ops-first, not AI-first. The product must reduce Shadowrun bookkeeping, reduce duplicate entry, support cross-device play, explain rules with provenance, preserve house-rule flexibility, and avoid hosted lock-in.

## Your part
EA should shape the AI/skill strategy so the GM Companion is a grounded copilot on top of Hub, not the product center.

Prioritize:
- skill contracts that operate on runtime facts, evidence pointers, explain traces, approvals, and draft-first behavior
- product/roadmap guidance that keeps P0 centered on Player Play Shell, GM Ops Board, session ledger, Explain/Source, interop, and NPC reuse
- P1 guidance for prep workspace, encounter/mook tooling, recap/memory candidates, notes/checklists, and reveal/player surfaces
- P2 guidance that pushes cinematic or media-generation features after the session-ops layer is trusted
- concrete skill concepts such as rules_referee, spider_alert_triage, npc_pack_drafter, session_recap_candidate, and player_dispatch_draft

Guardrails:
- do not frame the product as "replace the GM with chat"
- do not allow AI outputs to become hidden rules truth
- do not propose hosted lock-in or mandatory connectivity as the default experience
- keep provenance, evidence, and operator approval central

Product implication:
The right story is: Hub is the Shadowrun session OS; the GM Companion is the grounded skill layer riding on top of it.
